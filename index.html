Agricultural Soil Types

Understanding your soil is the foundation of successful farming

Clay Soil

Heavy soil with high water retention but poor drainage. Ideal for certain crops when properly managed.

Texture: Fine
Drainage: Poor
Nutrients: High
Best for: Rice, wheat, cabbage

Sandy Soil

Light, warm, dry and acidic with low nutrients. Quick to warm up in spring but dries out fast.

Texture: Coarse
Drainage: Excellent
Nutrients: Low
Best for: Carrots, potatoes, lettuce

Loam Soil

A balanced mixture of sand, silt and clay. Considered ideal for most agricultural purposes.

Texture: Medium
Drainage: Good
Nutrients: High
Best for: Most crops, especially tomatoes, beans

Silt Soil

Intermediate between sand and clay. Fertile and holds moisture well but can compact easily.

Texture: Medium-fine
Drainage: Moderate
Nutrients: Medium-high
Best for: Most crops, especially grasses

Peat Soil

High organic matter content, dark and moisture-retentive. Usually acidic and low in nutrients.

Texture: Spongy
Drainage: Poor
Nutrients: Low
Best for: Root crops when improved

Chalky Soil

Alkaline soil with high calcium carbonate content. Often shallow and over chalk or limestone bedrock.

Texture: Variable
Drainage: Good
Nutrients: Low
Best for: Alkaline-loving plants
